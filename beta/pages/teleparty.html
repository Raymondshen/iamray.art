Summary

In 2018, I joined NetflixParty, which later became Teleparty in 2020, bringing together millions of users worldwide to watch movies and shows together on different streaming platforms. As the first designer on the team of 2, I've been in charge of creating the company's look, including its branding, website, extension, mobile, and digital assets since the beginning. In the early stages, even though we had ideas and features, the product needed things like layout, branding, and art direction. With the freedom to be creative, I worked closely with the founder on designing the product and deciding how it should look by studying the market. Over the years, we made many changes and improvements, and the images below show how the product's design has evolved from the first drafts to what it looks like now.

Process

During my design process, I delved into extensive research on Netflix Party's competitors. Through this exploration, I carefully compared, analyzed, and identified feature gaps, branding inconsistencies, and shortcomings in product flows. These issues were primarily attributed to a suboptimal user experience and the absence of effective user interface design and discoverability.

Identity

After brainstorming with the founder, we defined three keywords—connected, premium, and fun—guiding the company's identity. These influenced the consistent use of color, typography, and icons across all products.
