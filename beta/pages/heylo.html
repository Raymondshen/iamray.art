Heylo

As Heylo’s founding product designer, I led end-to-end design for the platform—defining its brand identity, building the design system, and shaping the user experience across mobile, desktop, and marketing channels.

Heylo helps community leaders manage groups of any size with tools for payments, event planning, group chats, documentation, and check-ins.

Through virtual and in-person research sessions, we learned how leaders run their communities, identified pain points, and uncovered opportunities to make Heylo more intuitive and impactful for both leaders and members.

Design System

We built a design system with a defined color palette, grid, and components to bring consistency and improve the overall user experience.

Groups

Since group leaders and community members are the core of Heylo, we focused on building key features to enhance every community onboarded to the platform.

Group Profile

Group profiles were key to community identity, and the redesign focused on capturing the vibe, showcasing members, sharing events, and clearly communicating what the group is about.

Leaders & Members Onboarding

We saw drop-offs and conflicts during onboarding, so we redesigned the flow to reduce inputs and simplify how leaders and members join groups.

Events

The original event form hid key features, so we redesigned it to include payments, roles, waitlists, size limits, and tickets—helping leaders streamline event creation and stay engaged on the platform.

We overhauled the event registration flow to support new features, letting users select and purchase tickets, add guests, handle payments, complete questionnaires, and add or share events to their calendars.

Chatroom

Beyond events, Heylo supported topics and event chatrooms for member interaction. I redesigned the chatroom layout to accommodate new features—emojis, photo uploads, polls, @mentions, roles—and improved the visual design and user experience.

Social Media

As a social platform, Heylo added features for members and leaders to share event-generated images directly to Instagram. I led the research and design for how users interact with and share these photos on both desktop and mobile.

Ai Image

Based on data and leader feedback, many events lacked quality cover images. To address this, we integrated AI-generated images, allowing users to create improved event visuals from short prompts, making events more engaging and appealing within each group.

App Store

With the app’s rebrand and new features, we redesigned the App Store screenshots to reflect Heylo’s fresh look, build trust, and strengthen brand identity for users.

The redesign of Heylo strengthened the platform’s usability and connection opportunities, delivering a seamless experience for both leaders and members.
